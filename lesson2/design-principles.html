Hick's Law

Flight Club

flightclub.com

Flightclub does a great job with the organization of their products. On their front page, they have different tabs on the navigation bar that separates the different brands of shoes. When a user has selected the brand, they have a list of different shoe names, such as Jordan 1. Once the user selects the name of the shoe, Flightclub displays all shoes under that name. On the left side of the page, there are a selection of filters that can be checked to find the desired item quicker. The image above displays Jordan 1 Shoes with the filter Brown and available shoe size of 9.5.

White Space and Clean Design

Squarespace

squarespace.com

Squarespace may have a lot of information on their website, but they incorporate just the right amount of whitespace to grab the user’s attention. One way they use whitespace is to divide sections apart. They do this to separate information, making each section look simple. Spaces between the images and the descriptions makes the content easier to read and look at.

PARC: Contrast

Apple

apple.com

Apple’s websites are all about contrast. With their new products out such as the vibrant iMacs and the purple iPhone 12, they create stunning visuals that make their products stand out even more. For instance, the image above captures an iPhone 12 with the green color. They use different color values of the iPhone on the font and background to create a focal point.
