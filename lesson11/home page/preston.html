The Gloomy Yet Comforting Trail Hike

by John Doe

It was a chilly morning, as expected in Preston Idaho. The air cold and the wind light, nature is begginning to wake up around this time. My parents and I decided to hike up the Green Mountain Trail, which conveniently started right next to our house. The trail was slow, yet calming. The crunching of leaves from each step we take, to the dense fog that is travelling above the redwood treetops. A sense of comfort could be felt throughout our journey. After ten minutes of walking, the fog suddenly dispersed, the trees began to clear up and we arrived at a bench overlooking the cliffside of the Mountain. And to our surprise, the golden sun began to wake up as well, giving us warmth and comfort. We all sat on the bench and silently starred into the sun. It was almost as if it was a dream, and tiny sprouts of tears came out of my eyes. I slowly closed my eyes and whispered to myself, "This is what I live for."
