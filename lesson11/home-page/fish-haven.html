The Spring Story

by Bill Brown

We arrived at the lake in the afternoon, and to our surprise the dock was vacant. I got a feeling that people came here earlier than we did to catch some fish. We proceeded to the fishing dock with our fishing equipment and began to set it up. As we sat down on our chairs after we casted our fishing poles, the still silence of the lake came over our shoulders and into our ears. How peacful it was. Taking in mother nature and her beauty: The clear fresh lake, the tall trees surrounding it, the old wooden dock that creeks with each step you take. This is what it's about.
